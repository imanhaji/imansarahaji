Meet Iman

I'm a student at the University of Washington entering my final year of my Bachelor's in Civil Engineering.
My passion for civil engineering stems from my work in SENSOL Systems, where I work to develop a prototype for a modular and solar-powered crosswalk that lights up as pedestrians walk across it in effort to improve public safety. I hope to work on projects that improve our community by enhancing safety and integrating sustainable energy sources and materials into infrastructure and construction. One of Seattle's missions is to make its built environment more walkable and accessible, and Washington aims to end traffic deaths and serious injuries by 2030. I aspire to contribute to the driving force of innovation that is developing technology and solutions to our transportation systems to ensure the safety and sustainability, particularly in low infrastructure areas. I aim to utilize my knowledge and experience in traffic, transportation, infrastructure, and construction to further develop accessibility and sustainability to the multimodal transportation system and the built environment.
